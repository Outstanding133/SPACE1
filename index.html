BLACK HOLE

A black hole is a region in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. It is formed when a massive star collapses under its own gravity.

Key Features:
  • Singularity – A point of infinite density at the center.
  • Event Horizon – The boundary beyond which nothing can escape.

Types:
  • Stellar Black Holes (formed from collapsed stars).
  • Supermassive Black Holes (millions to billions of times the Sun’s mass, found in galaxy centers).
  • Intermediate & Primordial Black Holes (hypothetical types).

Time Dilation – Due to extreme gravity, time slows down near a black hole.

Hawking Radiation – Theoretical radiation predicted by Stephen Hawking, suggesting black holes can slowly evaporate.
